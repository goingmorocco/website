In Morocco, short-term apartment rentals are common in major tourist cities such as Marrakech, Casablanca, Tangier, and Agadir. You can find modern apartments in city centers, traditional homes in medinas, or seaside apartments near beaches.

Many travelers visiting Morocco prefer to stay in an apartment instead of a hotel. Vacation rentals give you more space, privacy, and the chance to live like a local for a few days or weeks.

This guide explains **how vacation rentals work in Morocco**, what to expect before booking, and how to find reliable apartments for your stay in Marrakech or any other city.

## What Is a Vacation Rental in Morocco?

A vacation rental in Morocco is usually a **fully furnished apartment or house rented for short stays**. These properties are designed for tourists who want more flexibility than a traditional hotel.

Most vacation rentals include:

- A private bedroom or multiple bedrooms
- A living room
- A kitchen with basic equipment
- A bathroom
- Wi-Fi and air conditioning in many modern apartments

Some rentals also offer **balconies, rooftop terraces, swimming pools, or views of the medina or the ocean**. Apartments are especially popular among:

- Families traveling together
- Couples staying longer than a few days
- Digital nomads
- Travelers who prefer cooking their own meals

## Popular Cities in Morocco for Apartment Rentals

Vacation apartments are available across Morocco, but some cities have a larger selection and better services for tourists.

## Marrakech Rental Apartments: Medina vs Gueliz

Marrakech has the largest number of vacation rentals in Morocco. Many apartments are located in modern districts such as Gueliz, Hivernage, and Majorelle, while others are inside the old medina.

As a Moroccan, I would say the Medina vs Gueliz choice really does change your whole trip, not just your commute. Stay in the Medina if you want noise and atmosphere right outside your door every morning; stay in Gueliz if you want to sleep without earplugs.

If you plan to rent an apartment in Marrakech, the neighborhood you choose can shape your travel experience. Marrakech has both **historic districts and modern neighborhoods**, and each area offers a different type of stay.

Many travelers choose between **the Medina and Gueliz**, but there are also other areas worth considering depending on your travel style. Below is a simple overview of the most popular areas for apartment rentals in Marrakech.

### Marrakech Medina (Old City)

The **Medina of Marrakech** is the historic center of the city and a UNESCO World Heritage Site. Staying here puts you close to famous places such as **Jemaa el-Fna square, souks, and traditional riads**. Many of these stays follow the traditional [riad](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Riad) design, built around a central courtyard.

Apartments in the Medina are often located inside restored riads or traditional houses. The streets are narrow, and cars usually cannot reach the front door of the property.

**Why travelers choose the Medina**

- Close to major attractions
- Traditional Moroccan architecture
- Easy access to markets and local restaurants
- Authentic cultural experience

**Things to keep in mind**

- It can be busy and noisy in some areas
- You may need to walk with your luggage through narrow streets
- Buildings are usually older than in modern districts

### Gueliz (Modern City Center)

Gueliz is the **modern district of Marrakech**, built during the French protectorate period. It is known for wide streets, shopping centers, cafés, and modern apartment buildings.

Many vacation rentals in Gueliz are located in **new residential complexes with elevators, parking, and security**.

**Why travelers choose Gueliz**

- Modern apartments with newer facilities
- Easy access by car or taxi
- Many restaurants, cafés, and shops nearby
- Quieter than the Medina at night

**Things to keep in mind**

- It is about 10–15 minutes from the Medina by taxi
- The atmosphere is less traditional

## Renting Vacation Apartments in Casablanca

Casablanca offers modern apartments in high-rise buildings. Many travelers choose this option for business trips or short city stays.

From my experience helping travelers plan stops in Casablanca, most people are surprised by how spread out the city is. Picking the wrong neighborhood here costs you more time stuck in traffic than in almost any other Moroccan city.

Apartment rentals are common in Casablanca, especially in modern residential buildings. The city is large, so choosing the right neighborhood can make your stay more comfortable. 

Below are some of the most popular areas where travelers rent apartments in Casablanca.

### Anfa and Racine

The **Anfa and Racine districts** are among the most popular areas for vacation rentals in Casablanca. These neighborhoods are modern, safe, and well located. 

Many apartments here are inside residential buildings with elevators, security, and underground parking.

**Why travelers choose Anfa and Racine**

- Central location
- Modern apartment buildings
- Many restaurants, cafés, and bakeries
- Easy taxi access to other parts of the city

These areas are a good choice for both **tourists and business travelers**.

### Maarif

Maarif is one of Casablanca’s busiest districts and a major **shopping and commercial area**. Many travelers stay here because everything is close by.

You will find shopping malls, international restaurants, and many services within walking distance.

**Why travelers choose Maarif**

- Central location
- Good public transport connections
- Many shops and restaurants
- Lively city atmosphere

However, traffic can be heavy during peak hours.

### Ain Diab (Beachfront Area)

Ain Diab is located along the Atlantic coast and is known for its **beachfront apartments, seaside cafés, and restaurants**. Many apartments in this area offer **ocean views or quick access to the beach**.

**Why travelers choose Ain Diab**

- Close to the beach
- Relaxed atmosphere compared to the city center
- Popular restaurants and beach clubs
- Nice sunset views over the ocean

This area is ideal for travelers who want to **stay near the sea while visiting Casablanca**.

### Casablanca City Center

The historic city center around **Boulevard Mohammed V** and nearby streets offers apartments close to traditional markets and colonial-era buildings.

This area is convenient for travelers who want to explore Casablanca’s historic side and visit places like **the Hassan II Mosque**.

**Why travelers choose the city center**

- Close to major landmarks
- Historic architecture
- Easy access to public transport
- Many local cafés and shops

Some buildings here are older than those in newer districts like Anfa or Maarif.

### Comparison of Casablanca Apartment Areas

Casablanca has a wide range of apartments for short stays, from **modern city apartments to beachfront rentals**. The best area for you depends on your travel plans and whether you prefer staying near the **business districts, the beach, or historic landmarks**.

## Renting Apartments in Tangier

Tangier is one of the most unique cities in Morocco. Located between the **Mediterranean Sea and the Atlantic Ocean**, the city combines historic neighborhoods, modern districts, and seaside areas.

Many visitors I have spoken with assume Tangier is just a ferry stop on the way to Spain, but the city itself, especially the Kasbah and Marshan, deserves at least two full days on its own.

Apartment rentals are common in Tangier, especially in neighborhoods near the **beach, the city center, and the historic medina**. Each area offers a different experience depending on what type of stay you prefer.

Below are some of the most popular neighborhoods for vacation rentals in Tangier.

### Tangier Medina and Kasbah

The **Tangier Medina** is the historic heart of the city. It is a maze of narrow streets filled with traditional markets, cafés, and historic buildings.

At the top of the medina sits the **Kasbah**, a historic fortress area with panoramic views of the sea and the port. Many traditional homes and riads have been restored into guesthouses and apartments.

**Why travelers choose the Medina and Kasbah**

- Historic atmosphere and traditional architecture
- Walking distance to markets and local cafés
- Close to major landmarks like Grand Socco and the port
- Unique riads and boutique apartments

**Things to keep in mind**

- Streets are narrow and often pedestrian-only
- Some buildings are older than those in modern districts
- Access by car can be limited

### Malabata and the Corniche (Beachfront Area)

Malabata is one of the most modern areas in Tangier. It is located along the **Mediterranean coastline** and is known for its beach, marina, and modern residential buildings. Many apartments here are located in **new residential complexes with sea views**.

**Why travelers choose Malabata**

- Modern apartments with elevators and parking
- Direct access to the beach and seaside promenade
- Many restaurants, cafés, and beach clubs nearby
- Popular nightlife and ocean views

This area is ideal for travelers who want **a modern stay near the sea**.

### Marshan

Marshan is one of Tangier’s most elegant residential neighborhoods. It sits on a hill between the medina and the modern part of the city. The area is known for its **villas, gardens, and quiet streets**, while still being close to the historic center.

**Why travelers choose Marshan**

- Quiet and upscale residential area
- Beautiful views of the city and coastline
- Close to the Kasbah and historic attractions
- More relaxed atmosphere than the city center

This neighborhood is a good choice for travelers who want **a peaceful stay while remaining close to the medina**.

### Tangier City Center (Ville Nouvelle)

The **modern city center**, often called _Ville Nouvelle_, is the commercial heart of Tangier. It is located around **Boulevard Pasteur and Place de France**. Here you will find many apartment buildings, shops, cafés, and restaurants.

**Why travelers choose the city center**

- Central location with many services
- Good transport connections
- Walking distance to shops and restaurants
- Easy access to taxis and the train station

This area is convenient for travelers who want **a practical location close to everything**.

### Quick Comparison: Tangier Apartment Areas

Tangier offers a wide range of apartment rentals, from **traditional homes in the medina to modern beachfront apartments overlooking the Mediterranean**.

## Best Areas to Stay in Fes Apartments

Fes is one of Morocco’s oldest and most historic cities. Known for its **ancient medina, traditional crafts, and historic mosques**, the city attracts travelers interested in culture and history.

Locals in Morocco often say you cannot really understand Fes until you have gotten a little lost in the medina at least once. Staying inside the walls, even for one night, gives you a very different feel for the city than a quick day visit.

Apartment rentals in Fes are available in both the **old medina and the modern districts** of the city. Each area offers a different experience depending on whether you want to stay close to historic landmarks or prefer modern buildings with easier access by car.

Below are some of the most popular areas for renting apartments in Fes.

### Fes Medina (Fes el-Bali)

The **Medina of Fes**, also known as _Fes el-Bali_, is one of the largest historic medinas in the world and a UNESCO World Heritage Site.

Apartments here are often located inside **traditional riads or restored historic homes**. Staying in the medina allows visitors to experience the historic atmosphere of the city and walk to many famous sites.

**Why travelers choose the Medina**

- Close to historic attractions and markets
- Traditional Moroccan architecture
- Walking distance to many landmarks
- Unique riad-style apartments

**Things to keep in mind**

- Streets are narrow and mostly pedestrian-only
- Buildings are often centuries old
- Access by car is limited

### Ville Nouvelle (New City)

The **Ville Nouvelle** is the modern part of Fes built during the French protectorate period. It offers wide streets, modern apartment buildings, and many shops and restaurants.

Most apartment rentals here are located in residential buildings with **elevators, parking, and easier access by taxi or car**.

**Why travelers choose Ville Nouvelle**

- Modern apartments and buildings
- Easy access to transportation
- Many cafés, restaurants, and shopping areas
- Quieter and more spacious streets

This area is a good option for travelers who prefer **modern comfort while visiting Fes**.

### Batha and the Medina Gates Area

The **Batha district** sits between the medina and the newer parts of the city. It is located near **Bab Boujloud (the Blue Gate)**, one of the main entrances to the medina.

Many small guesthouses, riads, and apartments are located here, offering easy access to both the historic and modern areas.

**Why travelers choose Batha**

- Close to the medina entrance
- Easy walking access to historic attractions
- More convenient access for taxis than deeper medina areas
- Many traditional guesthouses and apartments

### Saiss and New Residential Districts

The **Saiss area and nearby residential neighborhoods** are among the newest parts of Fes. Many modern apartment complexes have been built here in recent years. These neighborhoods offer larger apartments and a quieter environment compared to the busy medina.

**Why travelers choose Saiss**

- New apartment buildings
- More space and modern facilities
- Quiet residential atmosphere
- Good road access

This area is often chosen by **long-stay visitors or families**.

Fes offers a variety of apartment rentals, from **traditional riads inside the historic medina to modern apartments in the newer districts of the city**.

## Renting Vacation Apartment in Agadir

Agadir is one of Morocco’s most popular seaside destinations. The city is known for its **long sandy beach, modern hotels, and relaxed atmosphere**. 

The way I see it as a Moroccan, Agadir works best for travelers who actually want a beach holiday, not a culture-heavy trip. If sightseeing is your main goal, you will get more out of Marrakech or Fes.

Because Agadir was rebuilt after the 1960 earthquake, most neighborhoods have **modern buildings and wide streets**, which makes apartment rentals easy to find.

Many travelers choose apartments in Agadir for **longer stays, beach holidays, or winter visits**, as the city has a mild climate all year.

Below are some of the best areas to rent an apartment in Agadir.

### Agadir Beachfront

The area near **Agadir Beach and the seaside promenade** is one of the most popular places for tourists to stay. Apartments here are close to the beach, restaurants, cafés, and tourist services. Many buildings also offer balconies with **views of the Atlantic Ocean**.

**Why travelers choose the beachfront area**

- Walking distance to the beach
- Many restaurants and cafés nearby
- Easy access to the marina and tourist activities
- Beautiful ocean views

This area is ideal for travelers who want to **spend most of their time near the sea**.

### Agadir Marina

The **Agadir Marina** is a modern waterfront complex with restaurants, shops, and luxury apartments. It is located near the beach and close to the **Kasbah hill**, which overlooks the city.

Many apartments here are inside secure buildings with pools and private parking.

**Why travelers choose the Marina**

- Modern luxury apartments
- Close to the beach and marina restaurants
- Quiet and secure environment
- Nice views of the harbor and boats

The marina area is popular among travelers looking for **upscale holiday apartments**.

### Founty (Tourist Zone)

Founty is a modern residential district located between the city center and the beach. Many newer apartment complexes have been built here in recent years. This area is popular with both **tourists and expats**, and it offers many modern rental options.

**Why travelers choose Founty**

- New apartment buildings with modern facilities
- Quiet residential environment
- Short distance to the beach
- Good restaurants and supermarkets nearby

Founty is a good choice for travelers who want **modern apartments with more space and a quieter atmosphere**.

### Talborjt

Talborjt is one of Agadir’s **local city districts**. It offers a more everyday Moroccan atmosphere compared to the tourist areas. Apartment rentals here are often more affordable and close to local markets, bakeries, and cafés.

**Why travelers choose Talborjt**

- Lower rental prices
- Local neighborhood atmosphere
- Good access to shops and services
- Easy taxi access to the beach

This area is a good option for travelers who want to **experience daily life in Agadir while staying within budget**.

## Best Areas to Stay in Taghazout Apartments

Taghazout is a small fishing village located about **20 kilometers north of Agadir**. Over the past years, it has become one of Morocco’s most popular destinations for **surfing, beach holidays, and relaxed coastal stays**.

From my point of view as a local, Taghazout has changed a lot in the last few years. The village itself is still relaxed, but Taghazout Bay nearby is now closer to a resort town than a fishing village.

Many travelers choose apartments in Taghazout because they offer **ocean views, easy beach access, and a quiet atmosphere** compared to larger cities. The village and nearby areas now offer many short-term rentals, from simple surf apartments to modern seaside residences.

Below are some of the best areas to rent an apartment in Taghazout.

### Taghazout Village Center

The **center of Taghazout village** is the most popular place to stay for travelers who want to be close to surf spots, cafés, and small restaurants. Apartments here are often located in small buildings along narrow streets that lead down to the beach.

**Why travelers choose the village center**

- Walking distance to the beach and surf spots
- Many cafés, restaurants, and surf shops nearby
- Relaxed village atmosphere
- Beautiful sunset views over the Atlantic

**Things to keep in mind**

- Streets are narrow and parking can be limited
- Some apartments are simple compared to modern resorts
- The area can be lively during peak surf season

### Taghazout Bay

Taghazout Bay is a **new coastal resort area** located just south of the village. It includes modern apartment complexes, resorts, and golf courses. Many apartments here are located in **secure residential communities with pools and modern facilities**.

**Why travelers choose Taghazout Bay**

- Modern apartments and new buildings
- Quiet and organized environment
- Swimming pools and resort-style facilities
- Close to the beach and golf course

This area is ideal for travelers who want **modern comfort and a calm environment near Taghazout**.

### Tamraght

Tamraght is a neighboring village located about **5 minutes south of Taghazout**. It has become popular with surfers, digital nomads, and long-stay travelers.

Apartment rentals here are often **more affordable than in Taghazout**, and many properties offer balconies with ocean views.

**Why travelers choose Tamraght**

Tamraght is a good option for travelers who want **a quiet place near Taghazout but with more affordable apartments**.

### Aourir (Banana Village)

Aourir, often called **Banana Village**, is located about **10 minutes from Taghazout**. It is a larger local town with markets, bakeries, and many services. Apartments here are usually cheaper than those in Taghazout, making it a practical option for longer stays.

**Why travelers choose Aourir**

- Affordable apartment rentals
- Local markets and daily services
- Good transport connections to Agadir
- Easy access to surf beaches nearby

## Is It Legal to Rent Apartments to Tourists in Morocco?

Yes, vacation rentals are legal in Morocco, but property owners must follow certain regulations.

Many short-term rentals are registered as **tourist accommodations** and may require guests to provide identification when checking in.

**Some things to expect:**

- You may be asked for your **passport information**
- Some rentals charge a **tourist tax per night**
- Hosts may ask guests to respect building rules and quiet hours

Booking through a trusted platform helps make sure the property follows local regulations.

**Local Tip:** Ask your host directly whether the local tourist tax (taxe de sejour) is included in the price shown online or charged separately at check-in. Some listings show a lower upfront price and then collect this tax in cash on arrival.

## Tips for Booking a Vacation Rental in Morocco

Before booking an apartment in Morocco, it helps to check a few details.

1. **Look at recent reviews: **Reviews from other travelers can tell you if the apartment is clean, safe, and accurately described.
2. **Check the exact location: **Some rentals say “Marrakech Medina” but may be located outside the old city.
3. **Confirm the amenities: **If you need Wi-Fi, air conditioning, or a washing machine, verify this before booking.
4. **Ask about check-in arrangements: **Some hosts meet guests in person, while others provide a lockbox for keys.

## Things to Know Before Staying in an Apartment in Morocco

Vacation rentals are comfortable, but they work differently from hotels.

For example:

- Reception is usually **not available 24 hours**
- Cleaning may happen **only before and after your stay**
- You may need to **contact the host directly** if something is needed

Still, many travelers enjoy the **extra space and independence** that apartments offer.
