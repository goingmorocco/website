As a Moroccan, I can tell you the argan oil in products like this almost always traces back to cooperatives around Essaouira and Agadir, where it is traditionally extracted by hand, a slow process of cracking and grinding nuts that takes hours of work for a small amount of oil.

### Argan oil: the MVP from Morocco

Argan oil, from the kernels of the argan tree, is often called a miracle ingredient for hair. It has been used for years in many cultures for its natural powers. Here’s why it’s a star in this spray:

- **Softens dry hair**: It helps dry or rough ends feel smoother, which makes hair easier to brush and style.
- **Reduces frizz**: By adding needed moisture and a touch of protection, argan oil lowers the chance of hair puffing up or looking fuzzy in the air.
- **Adds shine**: The oil gives a soft glow, not a heavy shine. Hair looks healthy, not oily.
- **Light feel**: Unlike some oils that can make hair heavy, argan oil is fairly light. It absorbs quickly, so hair stays airy and not weighed down.

Because the oil comes from Morocco, a country long‑known for argan beauty products, it also gives a cultural connection many readers find interesting. It’s one of those ingredients that feels both natural and effective.

**Local Tip:** If you ever buy argan oil directly in Morocco rather than in a packaged product like this, real cosmetic-grade oil should be pale gold and lightly nutty-smelling, not deep amber or strongly toasted-smelling. That darker, toastier version is the culinary kind meant for dipping bread, not skincare.

## How to use it, so it works best

One reason people like this spray is how simple the routine can be. You don’t need steps that take a lot of time. Here’s a smooth way to use it:

1. **Wash hair** as you normally do.
2. **Gently squeeze out water** with a towel, but don’t rub hard.
3. **Hold the bottle about arm’s length away**; around 6 to 8 inches.
4. **Mist lightly** from the middle of your hair down to the ends. Try not to spray right at the roots if your hair is fine or gets oily easily.
5. **Comb through** with a wide‑tooth comb or your fingers. This spreads the spray evenly and removes any remaining small knots.
6. **Air‑dry** or use a blow dryer or other tools, your hair has a layer of protection now.

If your hair is very dry or thick, you can use a little more on dry hair later for an extra softness boost or to tame flyaways. But even for thick hair, a small amount can go a long way, just add it slowly, not all at once.
