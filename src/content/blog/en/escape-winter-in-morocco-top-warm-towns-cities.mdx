Winter is coming, and if you're looking for a warm and sunny spot to escape the cold, Morocco is the perfect pick. In this post, you'll find out why it's such a great winter destination and where to go to enjoy the sun and the beach activities.

For years, Morocco has been a go-to for those seeking a winter getaway. Thanks to its mild, sunny weather year-round, it's ideal for anyone looking to avoid the chill. The central region, in particular, is a hotspot for visitors wanting to enjoy warm weather and even hit the beach for a swim or surfing the waves. You can also check current [practical travel information from Morocco's tourist board](https://www.visitmorocco.com/en/useful-information) for up-to-date details.

Agadir and the surrounding towns are where you’ll want to be. While other areas might get chilly, Agadir stays warm and sunny. Spots like Taghazout and Tamraght are favorites among surfers, even in winter, thanks to their great weather and laid-back vibes.

Let's dive in to learn more about some of the top warm towns & cities in Morocco where you can go to enjoy the warmth during winter.

## Agadir

![escape winter in agadir](https://static.wixstatic.com/media/b51d6b_6cb57d8f961f45889fffb18ef4539c7d~mv2.webp)

Agadir is the perfect spot if you're looking to escape the cold this winter. This sunny coastal city in Morocco offers a warm and sunny atmosphere with temperatures that hover around the mid-20s °C (70s °F) even in the winter months.

There’s no shortage of things to do in Agadir. You can explore the local souks like souk El Hed to shop for traditional Moroccan goods. You can also enjoy fresh seafood at one of the many seaside restaurants, or take a hike up to the Agadir Oufella for stunning views of the city and coastline. Water sports like surfing and jet skiing are also popular if you're in for some thrill adventures.

Agadir has a modern vibe combined with rich Moroccan culture. This makes it a unique getaway to both exploring Morocco and enjoying the warmth. Another great thing about this city is its close location to some charming towns and spots like Taghazout and Tamraght.

## Taghazout

![escape winter in taghazout](https://static.wixstatic.com/media/nsplsh_59484547626f465a336738~mv2_d_5067_3442_s_4_2.jpg)

Taghazout is a small fishing village just north of Agadir that has become a favorite spot for surfers and beach lovers looking for a laid-back vibe. It’s the perfect escape if you're after some sun, sea, and relaxation. It has beautiful beaches and warm winter weather. The waves here are also great for surfing, and it doesn't matter if you're a beginner or more experienced. Taghazout is famous for surf classes.

If surfing’s not your thing, Taghazout still has plenty to offer. You can take it easy with yoga classes, chill out at one of the beachfront cafes, or explore the nearby mountains for a peaceful hike. The town is small and relaxed, so it’s a great place to unwind and enjoy the simple pleasures of life by the ocean.

Taghazout has a cool, bohemian atmosphere that draws in a mix of travelers. You'll meet surfers from around the world, as well as digital nomads and other visitors. The sunsets are amazing, and the village itself is full of charm, with colorful houses and local markets. In a nutshell, Taghazout is a perfect winter escape where you can surf, relax, and explore.

## Tamraght

Tamraght is a peaceful village just a little south of Taghazout, perfect for anyone looking for a quieter spot by the beach. It’s a hidden gem where you can enjoy stunning ocean views, warm weather, and a relaxed atmosphere away from the crowds.

Tamraght is also a great place for surfing, just like Taghazout, and has some fantastic surf schools for all levels. If you're a beginner, this is the perfect place to learn, but it's also ideal if you just want to chill on the beach and watch the waves while having a sunbathe.

Besides surfing, Tamraght is known for its cozy guesthouses, where you can relax, enjoy home-cooked meals, and connect with other travelers. Yoga retreats are also popular here, and they'll give you the chance to recharge in a beautiful setting. Plus, nature around is gorgeous, and it would be fabulous to go hiking and exploring.

Tamraght is small but full of charm. There are a few cafes and local shops where you can grab a bite to eat or pick up some handmade crafts. It’s the kind of place where you can slow down, enjoy nature, and soak in the laid-back coastal life.

## Imsouane

![escape winter in Imsouane](https://static.wixstatic.com/media/b51d6b_69a417ce76f74c9f946f1cdc9393a876~mv2.jpg)

Imsouane is a peaceful fishing village that’s perfect for those who want to avoid the crowds. It's known for its incredible surf and fresh seafood. It has one of the longest waves in Africa, which makes it a paradise for surfers. Even if you’re not into surfing, just sitting on the beach and watching the surfers do their thing is a relaxing experience. The village itself is quiet and laid-back.

Aside from surfing, Imsouane offers stunning coastal views and untouched beaches where you can take long walks or have a peaceful picnic by the sea. The local seafood is fresh and delicious. There are small restaurants offering tasty meals at affordable prices everywhere. enjoying a sunset over the ocean or chatting with friendly locals, Imsouane feels like a hidden gem far from the hustle and bustle.

Life in Imsouane is all about simplicity. You won’t find big hotels or fancy resorts here, just cozy guesthouses and surf camps where you can really connect with nature and other travelers. It’s the kind of place where you wake up to the sound of the ocean and go to bed after watching a sky full of stars. If you're looking for a quiet coastal escape this winter, Imsouane is the perfect spot to relax and recharge.

## Aourir (Banana Village)

![escape winter in aourir morocco](https://static.wixstatic.com/media/b51d6b_4ffaf71e704a4efda7126135c4514f00~mv2.jpg)

Aourir, also called "Banana Village" because of its banana plantations, is a small town just a few kilometers from Taghazout. It’s the perfect mix of beach and village life, where you can enjoy a relaxed atmosphere and still get a taste of local Moroccan culture. The town is famous for its bananas, which are sold fresh along the roadside, Wz recommend stopping by for a snack after a day at the beach.

Every Wednesday, Aourir hosts a lively weekly market (souq) where you can find everything from fresh fruits and vegetables to handmade crafts and traditional Moroccan goods. It’s a great place to explore, shop for unique souvenirs, and experience the local way of life. The market is a must-visit if you’re in the area, and it’s a fantastic way to spend a day strolling through the stalls.

Even though Aourir is small, it has some lovely cafes and local eateries where you can enjoy a casual meal. The friendly vibe of the town and its close proximity to Taghazout make it a great place to stay if you want to avoid the more touristy areas. Whether you’re here for a day trip or a longer stay, Aourir offers a warm and authentic Moroccan experience that feels a bit off the beaten path.

## Tamri

![escape winter in tamri](https://static.wixstatic.com/media/nsplsh_ba007dcb0db64767b302eb9fee23f039~mv2.jpg)

Tamri is another small village near Agadir, famous for its delicious bananas and beautiful, wild beaches. It’s the perfect spot for anyone looking to experience a quiet, authentic side of Morocco. The beach at Tamri is known for its large dunes. It's almost like a combination of the Sahara and the sea. And by the way, Tamri is also a good spot for beginner surfers. Be careful though, beaches around Tamri could be dangerous sometimes due to rocks and even sharks (rarely).

One of the unique things about Tamri is the banana plantations. The village is well-known for this. You’ll find delicious bananas being sold fresh all around the area. Taking a walk through the banana groves and seeing the local agriculture up close is a special experience that gives you a feel for the simple life in this region. Oh! And the quality of the bananas is good.

Tamri is a great place to visit if you want to explore more rural areas. The village itself is quiet, with friendly locals and a few small cafes where you can enjoy a drink after a day of exploring. So, if you're in the mood for some peace and quiet, combined with a bit of beach time and local culture, Tamri is definitely worth the visit!

Here you go! These are the best locations in Morocco where you can escape winter. They're warm, sunny, relaxing, and have many activities to offer.
