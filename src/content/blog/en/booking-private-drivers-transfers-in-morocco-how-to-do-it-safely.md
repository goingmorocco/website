## The Real Transport Problems Tourists Face in Morocco

Many tourists think taxis will solve everything. But in reality, taxis in Morocco work differently depending on the city. Some use meters, others don't. Some refuse short rides, others overcharge tourists who don't know the going rate.

**Local Tip:** In Moroccan cities, a petit taxi only covers trips inside that city and is usually metered or has a fixed local rate, while a grand taxi (often an older Mercedes, shared with other passengers) is used for trips between towns. Petit taxi colors differ by city, red in Casablanca, blue in Marrakech, for example, so the color you saw in one city won't mean the same thing in the next.

### Common Taxi Problems Tourists Run Into

- Having to negotiate every price
- Not knowing if the price is fair and thus ending getting overcharged or scammed
- Drivers refusing long-distance trips
- Confusion at airports and train stations

For longer routes like Casablanca to Marrakech, Marrakech to Fez, or trips to the Sahara and Atlas Mountains, taxis are often not a realistic option at all.
