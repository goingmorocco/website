Hey there! Spring, from March to May, is one of the best times to visit Morocco. The weather feels just right. It is not too hot like summer and not cold like winter. Days are sunny and comfortable, so you can walk a lot without feeling tired. You can also check current [practical travel information from Morocco's tourist board](https://www.visitmorocco.com/en/useful-information) for up-to-date details.

Spring also makes Morocco look fresh and alive. Valleys turn green, flowers bloom everywhere, and snow in the mountains melts into rivers and waterfalls. This is a great season for hiking, spending time outdoors, and exploring slowly without stress.

In this guide, I will share the best places to visit in Morocco during spring. You will find lively cities, green nature spots, and quiet hidden gems. For each place, I explain why spring is a good time to visit, what you can do there, where to stay, and tips travelers often share after their trips.

## Why Visit Morocco in Spring

Spring offers a great balance. Daytime temperatures usually stay between 20 and 25°C (68–77°F), which makes sightseeing easy and pleasant. Nature looks its best, and popular places are not as crowded as in summer.

You can walk around cities without rushing and enjoy nature without extreme heat. Hotels and tours are often cheaper than in peak summer months. If you are traveling alone, with family, or as a couple, spring makes everything feel calmer and more enjoyable.

If you ask me, this is hands-down the season I'd recommend to first-time visitors who want to see Morocco without fighting crowds or heat.

## Marrakech in Spring: Gardens, Medina, and Day Trips

Marrakech is one of the most visited cities in Morocco, and spring is a perfect time to see it. The weather stays warm but comfortable, around 24°C (75°F). You can explore the medina, souks, and historic sites without feeling exhausted.

In spring, gardens like Majorelle Garden and Menara Garden are full of color and feel peaceful. Evenings in Jemaa el-Fnaa square are lively but more comfortable, so you can enjoy food stalls and street shows without heavy heat.

Marrakech is also a great base for day trips. Many travelers use it to visit the Atlas Mountains or the Ourika Valley for a break from the city.

**Best places to stay:** Riads inside the medina, such as Riad Atlas Palace and Spa or Riad DabaKech, are calm and traditional.

**Things to do:** Explore the souks, visit Bahia Palace and El Badi Palace, enjoy Moroccan food, relax in a hammam, and take day trips nearby.

Many travelers say spring lets them enjoy Marrakech fully, without the stress of summer heat or large crowds.

Many travelers I've spoken with say spring is when Marrakech finally feels walkable, no need to dash between shaded spots every few minutes like in July.

## Fes in Spring: Historic Medina, Tannery, and Culture

Fes is Morocco’s cultural and historical heart. Its old medina is one of the largest in the world, filled with narrow streets, workshops, and historic buildings. Spring temperatures, around 20–22°C (68–72°F), make long walks much easier.

In spring, Fes feels busy but manageable. You can visit tanneries, madrasas, and markets at a relaxed pace. If you visit in May, you might catch the Fez Festival of World Sacred Music, which adds a special atmosphere to the city.

**Best places to stay:** Traditional riads inside the medina, like Riad Fes Lile or Riad Rcif, often have great rooftop views.

**Things to do:** Visit the tanneries, Bou Inania Madrasa, old souks, and historic city gates. Take your time and enjoy the details.

Some travelers say Fes can feel intense, especially with sellers, but spring weather helps a lot, and a local guide makes the visit smoother.

## Chefchaouen in Spring: Blue Streets and Rif Mountains

Chefchaouen, known as the blue city, looks even more beautiful in spring. The Rif Mountains around it turn green, which makes the blue streets stand out even more.

This city is perfect if you like slow travel. You can walk through quiet alleys, take photos, and sit in small cafés without feeling rushed. Spring weather keeps the city calm and pleasant.

Nature around Chefchaouen is also at its best. Trails, waterfalls, and mountain views are fresh and easy to enjoy.

**Best places to stay:** Small guesthouses and riads like Dar Kisania or Riad Bin Souaki.

**Things to do:** Walk around the blue medina, shop for handmade items, enjoy mountain views, and take a day trip to Akchour.

Many travelers say spring is the best season here because the mix of blue streets and green nature feels magical.

## Essaouira in Spring: Beaches, Medina, and Sea Air

Essaouira is a relaxed coastal town with fresh air and a laid-back feel. Spring temperatures stay mild, around 20–22°C (68–72°F), with a light breeze that keeps things comfortable.

You can walk along the old city walls, watch fishermen at the port, and enjoy long beach walks. The medina is smaller and easier to explore than in big cities, which many travelers appreciate.

**Best places to stay:** Riads like Riad Dar Sebta or Riad Al Manara, close to the medina or the beach.

**Things to do:** Beach walks, eating fresh seafood, visiting the port, wind sports, and exploring art galleries.

People often describe Essaouira as a calm break from busier cities, especially enjoyable in spring.

## Merzouga and the Sahara Desert in Spring: Camel Rides and Stargazing

Spring is one of the best times to visit the Sahara Desert. Daytime temperatures stay warm but manageable, around 25–30°C (77–86°F), and nights are cool and comfortable.

Camel rides across the dunes feel easier in spring, and the clear skies make stargazing unforgettable. The desert feels peaceful, and crowds are smaller than in summer.

**Best places to stay:** Desert Villa Boutique Hotel, Merzouga Luxury Camps, or Paradise Luxury Camp.

**Things to do:** Camel trekking, sandboarding, watching sunrise and sunset, stargazing, and enjoying Berber music at night.

Many travelers say spring offers the perfect balance of warm days and cool nights in the desert.

From my experience helping travelers plan desert trips, spring really is the sweet spot here. Summer nights in the Sahara barely cool down at all, while spring still gives you that cold-night, warm-day contrast people come for.

## Agadir in Spring: Beaches, Promenade, and Family Travel

Agadir is a good choice if you enjoy beaches and a relaxed city. In spring, the weather is sunny and pleasant, and the long beach is perfect for walking and relaxing.

The city feels quieter than in summer, which makes it great for families or travelers who want an easy trip. The promenade is clean and safe, and cafés are nice for slow afternoons.

**Best places to stay:** Pickalbatros Palais Des Roses, beach resorts, or central hotels.

**Things to do:** Beach walks, surfing, sitting in cafés, and visiting Agadir Oufella for views.

Travelers often say Agadir feels simple and stress-free, especially in spring.

## Atlas Mountains in Spring: Hiking, Villages, and Nature

The Atlas Mountains are at their best in spring. Snow melts into rivers, valleys turn green, and villages feel alive with farming and daily life.

Spring is ideal for hiking because the weather stays cool and paths are safe. You also get a closer look at Berber culture and mountain life.

**Best places to stay:** Kasbah Tamadot, Dar Imlil, Toubkal Lodge, or Riad Atlas Prestige.

**Things to do:** Hiking, village walks, local markets, and exploring valleys.

Many travelers say spring hikes here feel peaceful and rewarding, with fresh air and beautiful views.

**Local Tip:** Snowmelt means river crossings on some Atlas hiking trails can run higher and faster than expected in early spring. If you're hiking near Imlil or Ourika in March, ask locally about water levels before crossing, rather than assuming a trail is as easy as it looks in photos.

## Ifrane in Spring: Green Town, Forests, and Relaxation

Ifrane is one of the greenest towns in Morocco and is often called “Little Switzerland.” In spring, parks and cedar forests become fresh and full of life.

The town is clean, quiet, and perfect for relaxing. Forest walks are easy, and spotting monkeys in nearby cedar forests is common.

**Best places to stay:** Michlifen Resort and Golf, Hotel Chamonix, or Zephyr Ifrane.

**Things to do:** Forest walks, picnics, cycling, and relaxing outdoors.

People love how spring makes Ifrane feel calm, fresh, and perfect for a nature break.

## Ourika Valley in Spring: Waterfalls, Hiking, and Village Life

Ourika Valley is a popular escape from Marrakech. In spring, rivers are full, hills turn green, and temperatures stay cooler than the city.

Many people visit as a day trip, but staying overnight lets you enjoy quiet mornings and evenings by the river.

**Best places to stay:** Dar Ikalimo, riverside guesthouses, or eco-lodges.

**Things to do:** Visit waterfalls, hike, eat by the river, and explore small villages.

Travelers often say the valley feels refreshing and peaceful, especially after time in busy Marrakech.

## Akchour in Spring: Waterfalls, Nature Hikes, and Day Trips

Akchour is a hidden gem near Chefchaouen, known for waterfalls, forests, and easy hiking paths. Spring brings strong water flow and pleasant temperatures.

The area feels fresh and natural, making it great for a day in nature.

**Where to stay:** Most visitors stay in Chefchaouen and go to Akchour for the day.

**Things to do:** Hiking, swimming in natural pools, taking photos, and relaxing in nature.

Many travelers say the hike is not too hard and very rewarding, with views, monkeys, and picnic spots.

## Bin El Ouidane in Spring: Lake Views, Boating, and Peaceful Escape

Bin El Ouidane is a quiet lake in the Middle Atlas. In spring, the water looks clear, and the surrounding hills turn green. It is a perfect place if you want to slow down and enjoy nature without crowds.

**Best places to stay:** Widiane Suites and Spa or lakeside lodges.

**Things to do:** Boat rides, kayaking, lakeside walks, and relaxing with mountain views.

Travelers say the calm water and scenery make it one of the most relaxing spots in Morocco.

## Is Spring the Best Time to Visit Morocco?

Spring is one of the best seasons to explore Morocco. The weather feels comfortable, nature looks alive, and places feel less crowded than in summer.

If you plan your trip in spring, you can expect comfortable weather, green landscapes, and a noticeably calmer pace than peak summer.
