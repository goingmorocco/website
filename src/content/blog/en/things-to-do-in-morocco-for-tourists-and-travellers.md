Hey there! Today, through this post, I’m going to take you on a virtual trip to Morocco, one of the most exciting and diverse destinations in the world. If you’re planning a visit, or just dreaming about it, you’re in the right place. Morocco has something for everyone, from outdoor adventurers to culture lovers and foodies. So, let’s dive into the best things to do in Morocco. For more official travel inspiration, check out the [Moroccan National Tourist Office](https://www.visitmorocco.com/en).

## 1. Camel Trekking in the Desert

Let’s start with an iconic experience—camel trekking in the Sahara Desert. Imagine riding a camel across endless golden dunes, watching the sunset turn the sky into shades of orange and pink. By night, you can camp under the stars in a traditional Berber tent, with nothing but the sounds of nature around you. Trust me, it’s a magical experience you’ll never forget.

You'll need to head to the Sahara Desert of southeastern Morocco. Specifically, you can visit Merzouga, a small village near the Erg Chebbi dunes, or M'Hamid el Ghizlane near the Erg Chigaga dunes. Both locations offer camel trekking, sunset views over vast golden sand dunes, and the chance to camp under the stars in traditional Berber tents. For broader context, [Morocco](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Morocco) has a rich and diverse cultural history worth exploring further.

## 2. Dive into Moroccan Culture and Food

Morocco is a melting pot of culture, and its food is no different. Tagine, couscous, mint tea—this is just the beginning. Every region has its own specialty, so don’t be afraid to try something new. And beyond food, the people here are warm and welcoming. Wander through the bustling souks, watch artisans craft their goods, and soak in the unique vibe of Moroccan life.

local markets, known as souks, are a great place to start. Cities like Marrakech, Fes, and Chefchaouen offer vibrant food stalls and cafes where you can sample authentic dishes prepared by locals. You can also [<u>join food tours</u>](https://www.goingmorocco.com/morocco-food-tours) to dive deeper into the flavors and history of Moroccan cuisine. Another tip is to visit family-owned restaurants or small eateries tucked away in less touristy areas for a more personal and traditional experience.

## 3. Get Active Outdoors

If you love adventure, Morocco won’t disappoint. Surf along the Atlantic Coast, especially in spots like Taghazout, known for its laid-back surfer vibe. Or head inland for some incredible hiking in the Atlas Mountains. Whether you’re climbing Mount Toubkal or strolling through lush valleys, the views are breathtaking.

For those looking for even more thrills, try quad biking through the dunes of the Sahara or mountain biking along winding trails. And don’t forget about the incredible rock climbing opportunities in areas like Todra Gorge – it’s a great way to challenge yourself while taking in the stunning surroundings.

## 4. Chill in Coastal Towns

Need some downtime? Morocco’s coastal towns like Essaouira and Taghazout are perfect for relaxing. Essaouira has this bohemian charm with its whitewashed buildings, ocean breeze, and artistic vibe. Taghazout, on the other hand, is the ultimate chill spot for surfers and beach lovers. Either way, you’ll leave feeling refreshed.

Beyond the beach, these towns also offer a laid-back cultural experience. Take a slow walk through Essaouira’s Medina, browsing the quirky local shops and sampling fresh seafood by the water. In Taghazout, kick back at a beachside café and sip mint tea while you watch the surfers ride the waves.

## 5. Enjoy Music and Festivals

Morocco loves to celebrate, and its festivals are a treat. From the Gnaoua World Music Festival in Essaouira to the Mawazine Festival in Rabat, there’s something for every music fan. And if you time your visit right, you might catch a traditional celebration or a local festival—it’s a fun way to dive deeper into the culture.

The music festivals are particularly amazing because they bring together artists from all over the world, blending traditional Moroccan rhythms with global sounds. If you’re in the mood for something more local, head to a village for a spontaneous drum circle or a folk music performance.

## 6. Camp in the Desert

Yes, I already mentioned camel trekking, but camping in the Sahara deserves its own spotlight. Picture this: cozy tents, traditional Moroccan meals, and live drumming under a starlit sky. It’s peaceful, it’s adventurous, and it’s one of the best ways to connect with Morocco’s natural beauty.

For the full desert experience, why not try glamping? Some campsites offer more luxurious setups with comfy beds, beautiful Moroccan rugs, and even private bathroom facilities. Wake up to the sunrise over the dunes, and in the evening, enjoy a traditional Berber meal around a campfire. It’s the perfect blend of comfort and adventure.

## 7. Stay in Luxurious Riads

If you’re looking for unique places to stay, Morocco’s riads are like nowhere else. These traditional houses often feature beautiful courtyards, intricate tilework, and rooftop terraces. They’re a perfect mix of comfort and authenticity. Some are even super fancy, making you feel like royalty during your stay.

The best part? Riads are often tucked away in the middle of the bustling medina, offering a peaceful escape from the busy streets. You can spend your days exploring the city and then retreat to your riad’s tranquil atmosphere in the evening. Many riads also offer spa services, so you can enjoy a traditional hammam (steam bath) or a relaxing massage to truly unwind.

## 8. Explore Old Cities and Historical Sites

Finally, you can’t visit Morocco without wandering through its ancient cities. In Marrakech, get lost in the medina and visit landmarks like the Koutoubia Mosque. In Fez, explore the oldest university in the world. Don’t forget to check out the Roman ruins of Volubilis or the blue streets of Chefchaouen. Every city has its own charm and history waiting to be discovered.

For a unique experience, head to Meknes and take a walk through the Bab Mansour gate, a stunning example of Moroccan architecture. In Rabat, explore the Kasbah of the Udayas and enjoy breathtaking views of the Atlantic Ocean. Morocco’s historical sites are so rich in culture, you’ll feel like you’ve stepped back in time with every turn.
