## Day-by-Day Itinerary: This Is The Plan

This 6-day Morocco tour follows a route that connects some of the country’s most well-known destinations. 

You'll move from the Atlantic coast to mountain towns, historic imperial cities, and the Sahara Desert before finishing in Marrakech. 

The itinerary below shows what you'll do and see each day, including the main stops, landscapes, and cultural sites along the journey.

### Day 1: Casablanca to Rabat to Chefchaouen

The tour begins in Casablanca. You'll start with a visit to the famous **Hassan II Mosque**, one of the largest mosques in the world.

After Casablanca, the journey continues to **Rabat**, the capital city of Morocco. Here you'll visit landmarks such as:

- **Hassan Tower**
- **Kasbah of the Udayas**

In the afternoon, the tour continues north to **Chefchaouen**: the  small mountain town with blue-painted streets and relaxed atmosphere.

You'll spend the night there and you'd be able to walk around the medina in the evening.

#### About Casablanca and Rabat

- **Casablanca**: Casablanca is Morocco’s largest city and economic center. Many tours start here because it has the country’s main international airport. The city is known for the impressive **Hassan II Mosque**, which stands on the Atlantic coast and is one of the largest mosques in the world.
- **Rabat**: Rabat is the capital of Morocco and a calm city compared to other major destinations. You'll get the chance to stop to see the historic **Hassan Tower**, the **Mausoleum of Mohammed V**, and the **Kasbah of the Udayas**, which overlooks the Atlantic Ocean.

### Day 2: Chefchaouen to Volubilis to Meknes to Fes

After breakfast, the trip continues toward the ancient Roman ruins of **Volubilis**. This UNESCO World Heritage site has well-preserved mosaics and historic remains from the Roman period.

The tour then stops in **Meknes**, where you can see the famous **Bab Mansour** and learn about the city's history as one of Morocco’s imperial capitals.

In the evening, you'll arrive in **Fes**, where you'll spend the night.

#### About These Destinations

- **Chefchaouen**: Chefchaouen is a small mountain town famous for its blue-painted streets and buildings. Located in the Rif Mountains, it has a relaxed atmosphere and narrow streets filled with local shops and cafés. It is one of the most photographed towns in Morocco.
- **Volubilis**: Volubilis is an ancient Roman archaeological site and a UNESCO World Heritage location. The ruins include old roads, arches, and detailed mosaics that show daily life during the Roman period in North Africa.
- **Meknes**: Meknes is one of Morocco’s four imperial cities. It became an important capital during the reign of Sultan Moulay Ismail in the 17th century. Visitors often stop to see **Bab Mansour**, one of the most famous gates in Morocco.

### Day 3: Guided Tour of Fes

The third day focuses on exploring Fes with a local guide. The city is home to one of the oldest medinas in the world. Important places often included in the tour are:

- **Al Quaraouiyine University**, considered the oldest continuously operating university
- **Bou Inania Madrasa**
- The traditional tanneries and craft workshops

You'll walk through narrow streets, markets, and historic buildings before returning to their riad for the night.

#### About Fes

- **Fes**: Fes is one of the oldest and most important cultural centers in Morocco. Its medina, **Fes el-Bali**, is a UNESCO World Heritage site filled with narrow streets, traditional markets, historic mosques, and craft workshops.

### Day 4: Fes to Ifrane to Ziz Valley to Merzouga Desert

This day includes a long but scenic drive toward the Sahara Desert.

The first stop is **Ifrane**, known for its alpine architecture and cool climate.

The journey continues through the Middle Atlas Mountains and cedar forests near Azrou before reaching the palm-filled **Ziz Valley**.

In the evening, you'll arrive in **Merzouga**. From there, you'll ride camels into the dunes of **Erg Chebbi** to reach a desert camp.

The night in the desert often includes dinner, music, and stargazing.

#### About Ifrane and Merzouga

- **Ifrane**: Ifrane is a small town in the Middle Atlas Mountains known for its clean streets, parks, and alpine-style buildings. Because of its cooler climate and European-style architecture, it is sometimes called the “Switzerland of Morocco.”
- **Merzouga**: Merzouga is a desert village located near the large sand dunes of **Erg Chebbi**. It is a popular base for Sahara desert experiences such as camel trekking, desert camps, and watching sunrise or sunset over the dunes.

### Day 5: Merzouga to Todra Gorges to Dades Valley

After sunrise in the desert, travelers return from the dunes and continue the journey. The route passes through **Erfoud** and reaches the impressive **Todra Gorge**, where high rock walls form a narrow canyon popular with visitors and climbers.

Later in the day, the tour continues to **Dades Valley**, where you'll spend the night in a hotel or riad.

#### About These Stops

- **Todra Gorge**: Todra Gorge is a narrow canyon with high rock walls that rise more than 300 meters. It is a popular stop for travelers and hikers because of its impressive scenery and the small river that flows through the valley.
- **Dades Valley**: Dades Valley is known for its dramatic landscapes, traditional villages, and winding mountain roads. The valley is also famous for its rock formations and palm groves, making it a scenic stop on desert tours.
- **Ait Ben Haddou**: Ait Ben Haddou is a historic fortified village made of earthen clay buildings. It is one of the best-preserved ksars in Morocco and has been used as a filming location for many movies and television series.

### Day 6: Dades Valley to Ait Ben Haddou to Marrakech

The last day of the tour takes you across the High Atlas Mountains.

Along the way, the route passes through the **Valley of Roses**, known for its rose products and annual festival.

One of the main highlights is **Ait Ben Haddou**, a famous fortified village and UNESCO World Heritage site that has appeared in many films.

The tour then crosses the **Tizi n'Tichka Pass** before arriving in **Marrakech**, where the trip ends.

#### About Ait Ben Haddou & Marrakech

- **Ait Ben Haddou**: Ait Ben Haddou is a historic fortified village made of earthen clay buildings. It is one of the best-preserved ksars in Morocco and has been used as a filming location for many movies and television series.
- **Marrakech**: Marrakech is one of the most visited cities in Morocco. The city is known for its lively **Jemaa el-Fna square**, historic palaces, traditional riads, and colorful markets. Many tours end here because it offers many hotels, restaurants, and cultural attractions.

## Travel Tips for This Route

Before starting a multi-day tour across Morocco, you should keep a few practical tips in mind.

- Distances between cities can be long, so expect several hours of driving on some days.
- Bring cash for small purchases in markets and villages.
- Always carry water during long drives and desert visits.
- Respect local customs, especially when visiting religious or traditional areas.

**Local Tip:** Bring small denomination dirhams for the stops in Ifrane, Azrou, and the villages around Erfoud. Card machines are rare outside the bigger cities, and shopkeepers in small towns often cannot break large notes.

These tips will help you prepare for the journey.
