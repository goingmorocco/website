If you are visiting Morocco or living there, you may quickly notice that Uber barely operates in the country. Uber stopped working in Morocco entirely in 2018, and relaunched in a limited way in late 2025, currently available only in Casablanca and Marrakech with licensed drivers. Outside those two cities, Uber still is not an option, and that's where inDrive has become the go-to replacement. inDrive has become one of the most popular ride-hailing options in the country.

[<u>**inDrive**</u>](https://indrive.tpk.ro/No9sxnjG) is widely used across Moroccan cities and is often described as the alternative to Uber in Morocco. It works through a mobile app and connects passengers directly with nearby drivers. Instead of fixed prices, the app allows users to suggest how much they want to pay for a ride. You can read more about how the app works on its [Wikipedia page](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/InDrive).

Because of this flexible system, [<u>**inDrive**</u>](https://indrive.tpk.ro/No9sxnjG) became popular with both locals and tourists. Many people find it easier to use than street taxis and more comfortable than buses, especially for short trips, airport transfers, or late-night rides.

Many travelers I've spoken with assume Uber just doesn't exist anywhere in Morocco, and for most of the country that's still close to true. The nuance, Uber being back in only two cities, is the part people usually miss.

## **What Is inDrive and How It Replaces Uber in Morocco**

inDrive is a ride-hailing app available on Android and iOS. Like Uber, it allows you to request a ride from your phone, see driver details, and communicate before pickup. The main difference is how pricing works.

With inDrive, passengers set the price first. Drivers can accept the offer or suggest a different amount. This system is attractive in Morocco, where taxi prices can sometimes feel unclear or inconsistent.

People who usually rely on Uber in other countries often feel comfortable using inDrive because:

- The app interface is simple
- Pickup and drop-off points are flexible
- You know the price before starting the trip
- There is no need to speak much French or Arabic

## **How to Use inDrive in Morocco (Step-by-Step for First-Time Users)**

If you have never used inDrive before, the process is very easy:

1. [<u>Download the inDrive app from Google Play or the App Store.</u>](https://indrive.tpk.ro/No9sxnjG)
1. Open the app and allow location access.
1. Type your starting point.
1. Enter your destination.
1. Suggest the price you are willing to pay.
1. Wait for drivers to respond to your request.
1. Choose a driver based on price, car, or rating.
1. Meet the driver and pay the agreed amount at the end of the ride.

**Useful tips:**

- Cash is the most common payment method.
- Message the driver to confirm the exact pickup location.
- Always check the license plate before entering the car.

## **Is inDrive Legal in Morocco?**

inDrive is not officially legalized under Moroccan transport regulations. Ride-hailing apps operate in a legal grey area because drivers usually do not hold professional transport licenses.

However, the app is widely used every day, especially in large cities. Many people rely on it as their main way to move around. While passengers rarely face issues, drivers may sometimes encounter problems with authorities.

Discussions about regulating ride-hailing services in Morocco are ongoing, and the situation may change in the future.
